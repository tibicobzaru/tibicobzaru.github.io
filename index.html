Vodafone Romania · 2022–2024

The store nobody was using correctly

Discover Graduate Programme, Business Analyst
Weekly 1-on-1s with Chief Commercial Officer

AllVodafone stores. Nationwide rollout.
+20%smartphone sales growth post-implementation.
+200%gadget category growth across the portfolio.
1,000+manual entries per day eliminated by automation.

Sales agents were describing products from memory. Chairs nobody sat in. Assets missing or placed wrong. The stores were set up to look good, not to help anyone actually buy something.

I walked the stores and talked to the frontline agents, not management. The issue wasn't people, it was environment. Products weren't physically available to touch. There was no one assigned to explain technology patiently to customers who came in for guidance rather than a pitch. I built the case and put it in front of the CCO through weekly meetings.

The concept rolled out across all Vodafone stores in Romania and a dedicated team was formed to handle implementation. The TechXpert role, a dedicated agent for customers who needed time and explanation, came directly from that work. Smartphone sales grew over 20% and gadget purchases exceeded 200% growth following the changes. The programme expanded internationally from there.

01

What kind of PM are you?

I work best on consumer products where the user is a real person, not a procurement committee. Specifically products where the interface does the explaining. No onboarding call, no implementation project, just the product working or not working.

02

What matters in PM work?

Realizing it’s an orchestration role: I focus on the who, what, and why, and stay open to new perspectives. I guide through questions and make sure my involvement adds value.

You built automations. How technical are you?

I can read code and have shipped things in Python, VBA, and UiPath. I taught myself Django to build a web app for a non-profit. Not an engineer, but I understand the work enough to collaborate with tech teams without being a pain.

03 · Case Study

Product thinking,
from brief to spec.

Based on a product challenge from an interview setting. Demonstration purposes only.

The Brief

Increase active platform participation without requiring admin involvement

An enterprise engagement platform wanted features that would keep end users coming back on their own — no prompts from a client admin, no manual campaigns. The challenge was to propose ideas, identify the strongest one, design a test, and write the spec for the tech team.

Idea Generation

1
Daily Top Performers Feed — an automated daily ranking of top-performing store units based on sales data submitted by managers. Self-updating, no admin effort after setup.
2
Peer Recognition Microfeed — employees send short recognition notes to colleagues directly in the app. Lightweight, social, requires nothing from admins once live.
3
Achievement Badge System — automated badges for consistent platform activity, sales streaks, and team milestones. Visible on employee profiles.

Competitor Research

Bonusly

Automated peer recognition embedded in daily work culture. Recognition works best when it is frequent, low-friction, and visible to the team.

Duolingo

Streak mechanics and leaderboards drive daily returns without human intervention. Social comparison and loss aversion do the work.

Salesforce Trailhead

Badges and ranked progression turn learning into a visible identity. People share their badges externally — in-product status extends beyond the platform.

Top Idea — Feature Spec

Daily Top Stores by Sales

A system that lets store managers log end-of-day register amounts. The platform automatically sums the total per store, filters for yesterday's submissions, and posts a ranked message to all employees showing the top three stores. No admin trigger required after initial setup.

Architecture Draft

Input→Manager enters register amounts. Store unit and date pulled from profile automatically.
DB Write→Amount, store unit, and timestamp written to shared database.
Filter→Daily cron: WHERE submissionDate = sysDate - 1, ordered by amount DESC.
Notify→Push notification to all employees with top 3 results. Clickable through to full leaderboard.
Edge Case→If no submissions found for previous day, notification is suppressed.

User Stories

Frontline EmployeeAs a frontliner, I want to know how my store compares to others so I can feel part of a competitive, high-performing team.
Store ManagerAs a store manager, I want to enter end-of-day totals quickly so my team sees where they stand without me communicating it manually.
CompanyAs a company, I want data entry and visibility restricted by role so sensitive performance information stays protected.

Acceptance Criteria

✓Data entry: Manager enters individual register amounts; system sums and stores the daily total automatically.
✓Automated notification: All employees receive a push notification each morning with yesterday's top 3 stores. No admin action required.
✓Access control: Data entry restricted to authorised managers. Analytics database read-only for all other roles.

Feasibility Test Plan

1

Select 5 stores from one existing client. Run a baseline engagement survey.

2

Before building, validate the data entry flow with a spreadsheet mockup — managers log amounts via dropdown, date auto-fills, sheet calculates totals.

3

Run the initiative live for one full work week.

4

Send a follow-up survey with at least one open qualitative question on perceived impact.

5

Repeat with 2 more clients before full rollout.

Success signals: higher engagement scores, positive perceived impact, managers willing to enter data consistently.

Data Entry Mockup

Rather than waiting for engineering to build the input layer, I prototyped the entire flow in Google Sheets and Zapier. Store managers select their store from a dropdown, the date auto-fills, and Zapier handles the rest: emails sent to the employee list without a single line of custom code.

Bonus Layer

What gets added once the core is validated

Extended Engagement Mechanics

Mysterious Special Mentions — 3 additional stores recognised daily by an undisclosed algorithm (app open frequency). Encourages employees to experiment with behaviour to crack it.
Top 3 Streak Badges — stores appearing in the top 3 for consecutive days earn a visible badge on employee profiles.
Store size clustering — separate leaderboards for small, medium and large stores so the ranking stays fair.

Inspired by in-group vs out-group dynamics and mechanics from Bonusly, Duolingo and Salesforce Trailhead.
